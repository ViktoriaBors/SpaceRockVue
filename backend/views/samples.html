Analogue and simulant samples

Analogue Samples

Analogues are representative of a large area of a target body without having well defined parameters. Good example is a basalt in our collection. The basalt is representative of the basalts present on the Moon.

Many of the field sites are termed as ‘analogue sites’, because they can be used to conduct field test in regard to the Moon (for example, rover field trials).

Simulants

Simulants are representative of a specific planetary body and lithology, with properties that more closely match those of the parent body. For example, a lunar mare regolith simulant would have a grain size distribution and grain shape similar to lunar regolith returned from the Moon during the Apollo missions.

Simulants can be ‘general use’ as previously described, or be more specific and aim to represent a particular sample or localized region on the Moon surface.

Borrowing materials

Borrowing both analogues and simulants is possible if you contact us. As they are loans, the material needs to be returned to the collection once the research is finished.

For small-scale test, it is easily possible to provide the materials. For large-scale test, it might be not possible to provide enough material.

Please note as well that some of our samples are ‘voucher specimens’. That means we have a really small amount for in-house test.

There can be several reasons for a particular sample cannot be loaned:

  • It is already on loan.
  • It has some health and safety concerns
  • We are concerned that the sample may be altered or lost during the delivery and loan period.
  • The reason requesting the sample is not justifiable base on the scientific application.

Approving procedure

Please NOTE it is possible to get your simulants approved. It takes the following steps:

  • Contact us via mail with the details about the material.
  • Register on our website under contact.
  • After login, you can add, modify and delete your samples. Some parameters of the sample are required (for example a name, short description and mineral composition).
  • Meanwhile please send us a small amount of the sample for further characterization and research in-house at SpaceRock.
  • After all the in-house laboratory analysis checks out (and matches with your results), the status of the sample changes from pending to approved.

These procedure ensure that the composition of the sample are well-described and follows all standards.
